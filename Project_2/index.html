Drinking Water Services VS Safe Sanitation

Top Country attributed to Unsafe WASH

*Tips: Use the slider to see how it changes across years!

The scatterplot above shows a positive correlation. Most of the countries have a good ratio of using both drinking water services and safe sanitation, except for the countries labelled in red.

*One emoji represents 10 deaths.

Chad, is known as the country with the highest mortality rate attributable to unsafe WASH. Based on unicef, only one in ten has access to improved sanitation. For male, there is a mortality rate of 100 out of 100,000. This also means that there will be 100 deaths out of the 100,000 population. For female, the mortality rate is 90 per 100,000 population. It is a very concerning issue that should be addressed.

Both dotplots shows that the population using basic sanitation gain throughout the years. Comparing urban area to rural, every region has higher percentage in urban. Also, Europe has the highest population which uses basic sanitation while Africa has the lowest in both areas.

Mortality Rate attributed to Exposure of unsafe WASH Services Per 100,000 Population of Year 2016

*Tips: Hover over each country to highlight it!

*Fun fact: Mortality rate is attributed based on population size, not percentage! For example, country Chad has a mortality rate of 101. This means that there will be 101 deaths out of each 100,000 population, instead of 101%.

According to CDC, unsafe WASH refers to the exposure of unsafe water, unsafe sanitation and lack of hygiene. From the size of circle plotted on map, it is shown that Africa has the highest mortality rate of unsafe WASH while Europe has the lowest.

With the fact that nearly 45% of people in Africa will face unclean sanitation condition, it is explainable why Africa has the highest mortality rate.
